# Inclusive Language Style Guide

A practical reference for 187INCLUDE reviews. Use this guide to flag loaded terms, suggest replacements, and explain the rationale to content owners.

## Core principle

Center the person and their stated identity. When in doubt, ask, offer options, and let people self-describe.

## Gender and pronouns

| Avoid | Prefer | Why |
|---|---|---|
| "both genders" | "all genders" | Erases nonbinary and gender-diverse people. |
| "opposite sex" | "another gender" | Binary framing. |
| "preferred pronouns" | "pronouns" | Pronouns are not a preference; they are a fact of identity. |
| "he or she" | "they", "the person", restructure | Singular they is grammatically correct and inclusive. |
| "ladies and gentlemen" | "everyone", "folks", "guests" | Gender-neutral greeting. |
| "mankind" | "humanity", "people" | Gender-neutral collective. |
| "maiden name" | "birth name", "former name" | Neutral and respectful. |

## Names and privacy

- Use the name a person currently uses, even if it differs from legal records.
- Avoid displaying "legal name" unless a legal or compliance process genuinely requires it.
- If a legal name is required, label it clearly, explain why, and limit visibility.
- Never expose a former name without explicit consent.

## Race, ethnicity, and culture

| Avoid | Prefer | Why |
|---|---|---|
| "minorities" (as a noun) | "people of color", "underrepresented groups" | "Minority" can imply lesser status and varies by region. |
| "urban" as a proxy | Be specific about geography or demographics | Often coded. |
| "exotic" | "distinctive", "specific to", or name the origin | Othering. |
| "pow-wow" for a meeting | "meeting", "discussion" | Appropriative. |

## Disability

Coordinate with 187ACCESS+. Default to community-preferred terms:

- "disabled people" and "people with disabilities" are both used; prefer the term the person or community uses.
- Avoid "differently-abled", "special needs", or "handicapped" unless quoting a self-identifier.

## Socioeconomic status

| Avoid | Prefer |
|---|---|
| "the poor" | "people with low incomes" |
| "underprivileged" | "under-resourced" |
| "cheap" (when describing people) | "affordable", "low-cost" |

## Age

| Avoid | Prefer |
|---|---|
| "the elderly" | "older adults" |
| "senior citizens" (unless preferred by audience) | "older people" |
| "millennials" as a lazy stereotype | Be specific about behavior or cohort data |

## Anti-bias checks

- Count who is shown as the default, expert, or decision-maker in examples.
- Avoid stereotypes in names, roles, and scenarios.
- Do not use identity as a plot twist or surprise.
- Flag tokenism: one diverse example in a sea of defaults is not inclusion.

## Evidence labels for claims

When the text makes claims about identity groups, label the evidence basis:

- `proved` — peer-reviewed or directly measured
- `measured` — analytics or survey data
- `modeled` — projection or estimate
- `inherited` — accepted community framing
- `interpreted` — analysis with acknowledged perspective
- `speculative` — hypothesis
- `unsupported` — remove or revise
