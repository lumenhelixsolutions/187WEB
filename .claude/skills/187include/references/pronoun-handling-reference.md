# Pronoun Handling Reference

A quick reference for collecting, storing, displaying, and communicating pronouns safely.

## Why pronouns matter

Pronouns are a core part of how people are referred to. Getting them right is basic respect; getting them wrong can misgender, exclude, or out someone in an unsafe context.

## Collection

- Ask for pronouns separately from gender.
- Make the field optional unless the system actively uses pronouns (for example, automated messages).
- Offer common options plus open text.
- Explain why the field exists: "So we can refer to you correctly in emails and community spaces."

## Common pronoun sets

| Subject | Object | Possessive | Reflexive | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| she | her | hers | herself | She sent her report. |
| he | him | his | himself | He sent his report. |
| they | them | theirs | themself | They sent their report. |
| ze | zir | zirs | zirself | Ze sent zir report. |
| xe | xem | xyrs | xemself | Xe sent xem report. |
| no pronouns | use name | — | — | Jamie sent Jamie's report. |

Do not treat this list as exhaustive. Always allow self-described pronouns.

## Display

- Show pronouns only where they add value and where the user consented.
- Common safe contexts: community profiles, conference badges, internal directories.
- Common risky contexts: public leaderboards, shared documents with external parties, customer support transcripts.
- Provide per-context visibility controls when possible.

## Defaulting and fallback

- If pronouns are unknown, use the person’s name or singular "they" — never assume based on name, photo, or voice.
- Automated messages should use the collected pronouns or default to the person's name.

## Correction workflow

- Make pronoun updates self-service.
- When a correction is shared, acknowledge it once, update systems promptly, and avoid making the person repeatedly remind others.
- Train support staff to accept corrections without debate or excessive questioning.

## Misgendering risk

Misgendering happens when a system or person uses the wrong pronouns or gendered language. Mitigations:

- Do not derive pronouns from gender fields.
- Do not derive pronouns from names or titles.
- Do not expose pronouns in contexts where they were not consented.
- Allow users to delete or hide pronouns at any time.

## Pronouns in generated content

When 187WRITE or other skills generate copy that refers to example people:

- Use a mix of pronouns across examples, or use neutral "they" consistently.
- Do not use pronouns as a signal of role, expertise, or personality.
- Avoid examples that contrast "he" as the expert and "she" as the user.
