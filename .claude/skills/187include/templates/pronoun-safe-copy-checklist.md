# Pronoun-Safe Copy Checklist

Use this checklist before publishing copy that refers to people by pronouns or gendered language.

## Before you publish

- [ ] Pronouns are collected with consent and stored privately by default.
- [ ] Generated or automated messages use the correct pronouns or fall back to the person's name.
- [ ] No pronouns are displayed publicly without explicit opt-in.
- [ ] Gender is not assumed from name, photo, voice, role, or title.
- [ ] Singular "they" is used when a person's pronouns are unknown.

## Language

- [ ] "Both genders" is replaced with "all genders" or "every gender."
- [ ] "Preferred pronouns" is replaced with "pronouns."
- [ ] "He or she" is replaced with "they" or restructured.
- [ ] Gendered greetings like "ladies and gentlemen" are replaced with "everyone" or "folks."
- [ ] Examples include a mix of pronouns or use neutral "they."

## Forms and fields

- [ ] Pronoun field is separate from gender field.
- [ ] Pronoun field is optional unless functionally required.
- [ ] Self-describe option is available for pronouns and gender.
- [ ] Title field is optional and includes neutral options.
- [ ] Legal name is justified, labeled, and restricted.

## Risk checks

- [ ] Deadname risk reviewed for migrations and historical data.
- [ ] Misgendering risk reviewed for automated communications.
- [ ] Search indexes and caches checked for outdated names or pronouns.
- [ ] Support staff trained to accept pronoun corrections.

## Sign-off

- [ ] 187INCLUDE review completed.
- [ ] 187ACCESS+ review completed if forms are involved.
- [ ] Content owner approved final copy.
- [ ] Affected-community reviewer signed off where relevant.
