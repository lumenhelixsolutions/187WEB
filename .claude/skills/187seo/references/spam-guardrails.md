# 187SEO Spam Guardrails

## Prohibited tactics

187SEO never recommends or tolerates the following. Each violates Google's spam policies and can result in ranking loss, manual actions, or site removal from search results.

| Tactic | Why it is prohibited | Google policy reference |
|--------|----------------------|------------------------|
| **Link schemes** | Buying, selling, or exchanging links to manipulate PageRank or rankings. Includes excessive reciprocal links, paid guest posts for links, and private blog networks (PBNs). | Google Search Essentials: Link spam policies |
| **Cloaking / hidden text** | Showing different content to users and search engines, or hiding text/links to manipulate rankings. | Google Search Essentials: Cloaking and hidden text |
| **Doorway pages** | Pages created to rank for specific queries that funnel users to a single destination without adding value. | Google Search Essentials: Doorway pages |
| **Scaled thin content** | Mass-producing low-value pages with little original content, often auto-generated or templated with only keyword swaps. | Google Search Essentials: Scaled content abuse |
| **Fake schema / fake reviews** | Adding structured data that misrepresents content, products, ratings, or reviews. Includes fabricated aggregate ratings or unverified reviews. | Google Search Essentials: Misleading structured data |
| **Bought reviews** | Paying for positive reviews or incentivizing reviews without disclosure, which also violates FTC guidelines. | Google Search Essentials: Fake engagement |
| **Unauthorized SERP scraping** | Scraping Google results or competitor pages at scale without permission or API access, violating terms of service. | Google Terms of Service / applicable laws |
| **Ranking promises** | Guaranteeing specific rankings, traffic, or timeline outcomes. Search rankings cannot be guaranteed. | Professional ethics / client honesty |
| **AI-search manipulation spam** | Creating or modifying content solely to manipulate AI overview or answer-engine summaries without providing genuine value. | Google Search Essentials / GEO best practices |

## Rejection script

When a request asks for a prohibited tactic, respond with:

> I can't help with that. It violates Google's spam policies and could result in a manual action or removal from search results. I can help you build sustainable, policy-aligned SEO instead — for example, a content strategy, technical audit, or outreach plan that earns links and trust naturally.

## Escalation

If a client insists on a prohibited tactic after being informed, document the request, decline implementation, and escalate to the project lead.
