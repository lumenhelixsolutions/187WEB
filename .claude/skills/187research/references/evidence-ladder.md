# Evidence Ladder

Use the ladder to classify every claim before it enters a lab artifact, demo, note, or publication. Always label the highest rung a claim can honestly occupy and provide the source route that supports it.

## Levels

| Rung | Label | Definition | Examples |
|------|-------|------------|----------|
| 5 | **Established theorem / law / meta-analysis** | Peer-reviewed, reproduced, and widely accepted by the relevant expert community. | Pythagorean theorem; Cochrane review with strong evidence. |
| 4 | **Peer-reviewed evidence** | Published in a reputable, peer-reviewed venue; methodology transparent. | Journal article with data and code availability. |
| 3 | **Preprint / computation / dataset result** | Public but not yet peer-reviewed, or derived from reproducible computation. | arXiv preprint; computational experiment with open code. |
| 2 | **Analogy / heuristic / expert opinion** | Reasonable pattern, analogy, or informed judgment without full verification. | "This resembles X"; practitioner guideline without RCT. |
| 1 | **Speculation / poetic framing / hypothesis** | Intentionally exploratory, narrative, or not yet testable. | "What if"; design metaphor; artistic concept. |
| 0 | **Disproven / retracted / unsafe to repeat** | Contradicted by stronger evidence or withdrawn by authors. | Retracted paper; deprecated clinical practice. |

## How to use

1. For each claim in the research output, assign a rung.
2. Cite the source route and date accessed next to the rung.
3. If a claim is promoted upward, require new evidence; do not promote by rhetoric.
4. When communicating to non-experts, translate the rung into plain language (e.g., "well-established", "early evidence", "educated guess").
5. Flag any medical/clinical/health claim at rung ≤3 for human/professional review.

## Examples

- "The Leech lattice is the densest sphere packing in 24 dimensions" → **Rung 5** (proven theorem, Fields Medal result).
- "This preprint reports a novel ADHD biomarker" → **Rung 3** (preprint; needs replication).
- "A consent-first support tool could reduce anxiety" → **Rung 2** (heuristic; plausible, not yet tested).
- "Imagine a lattice as a crystalline poem" → **Rung 1** (poetic framing).
- "Vaccines cause autism" → **Rung 0** (disproven and retracted).

## Proof vs pattern checklist

| Question | Rung if yes |
|----------|-------------|
| Is there a formal proof in a recognized venue? | 5 |
| Is there a peer-reviewed study with open data? | 4 |
| Is there a preprint or reproducible computation? | 3 |
| Is the claim supported mainly by analogy? | 2 |
| Is the claim exploratory or metaphorical? | 1 |
| Has stronger evidence contradicted the claim? | 0 |
