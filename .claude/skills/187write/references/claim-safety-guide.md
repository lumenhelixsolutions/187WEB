# Claim Safety Guide

How 187WRITE labels and rewrites public claims.

## Evidence ladder

| Label | Use when | Example phrase |
|---|---|---|
| proved | Replicated outcome with records | "In three controlled runs, X reduced load time by 40%." |
| measured | Directly tracked metric | "Our analytics measured a 12% click-through rate." |
| modeled | Calculation based on assumptions | "Modeled annual savings are $4,800 at current usage." |
| interpreted | Reasonable inference from data | "This pattern is interpreted as improved retention." |
| speculative | Possible but unverified | "One speculative path is a 20% conversion lift." |
| poetic/metaphorical | Figurative language | "Think of the router as a traffic conductor." |
| unsupported | No backing evidence | Flag and rewrite or remove. |

## High-risk claim categories

- Health or disability outcomes
- Financial or revenue results
- Legal interpretations
- SEO rankings or traffic
- Security guarantees
- Accessibility compliance

For high-risk claims, require:
1. Evidence label.
2. Source or methodology note.
3. Human expert review flag.

## Rewrite patterns

| Unsafe | Safe |
|---|---|
| "This will double your revenue." | "Modeled scenario: doubling revenue assumes X, Y, and Z." |
| "Guaranteed WCAG compliance." | "Aimed at WCAG 2.2 AA; formal audit required for compliance claims." |
| "The best stack for everyone." | "A strong fit for teams that value X and Y." |
| "Never get hacked." | "Reduces common attack surfaces; no system is unconditionally secure." |

## Checklist

- [ ] Every claim has an evidence label.
- [ ] High-risk claims include a review flag.
- [ ] Metaphors are labeled as metaphors.
- [ ] Unsupported claims are removed or rewritten.
