# Respectful Terminology Guide

Use this reference when 187ACCESS+ or 187INCLUDE writes about disabled people, access needs, or identity.

## Disability

Both are respectful when used by the person or community they describe:

- **Identity-first:** disabled people, Deaf people, autistic people.
- **Person-first:** people with disabilities, people who are blind, people with autism.

Default to the language preferred by the individual or community being discussed. When unknown, use person-first as a safe public default unless writing within a community that prefers identity-first.

## Access and impairment

| Preferred | Avoid |
|---|---|
| disabled people / people with disabilities | the disabled, handicapped, differently abled, specially abled |
| people with intellectual disabilities | mentally retarded, slow |
| blind and low-vision users | the blind |
| Deaf and hard-of-hearing users | hearing impaired |
| people with mobility disabilities | wheelchair-bound, confined to a wheelchair |
| neurodivergent users | mentally ill *(unless clinical context)*, abnormal |
| access needs | special needs |
| disabled community | victims of, suffers from |

## Avoid inspiration porn

Do not frame disabled people as inspirational solely for living ordinary lives. Focus on design, barriers, and agency.

## Person-first in examples

- Use real names or clearly fictional placeholders.
- Do not use disability status as a surprise twist or punchline.
- Ask for and use stated pronouns and names.
- Keep disability disclosure optional and consent-based.
