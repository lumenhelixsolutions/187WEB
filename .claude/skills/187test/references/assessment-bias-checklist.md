# Assessment Bias Checklist

Use this checklist before publishing any 187TEST instrument.

## Content bias

- [ ] No item assumes a specific nationality, religion, or culture.
- [ ] No item uses stereotypes for examples.
- [ ] Names in examples reflect diversity without tokenism.
- [ ] Scenarios do not assume a particular family structure or income level.
- [ ] Idioms and slang are avoided or explained.

## Construct bias

- [ ] Items measure the intended skill, not reading speed or vocabulary.
- [ ] Instructions are clear enough that format does not become the test.
- [ ] Visuals are optional or fully described.
- [ ] Audio items have text equivalents.

## Differential item functioning

- [ ] No item advantages or disadvantages a group unrelated to the skill.
- [ ] Distractors are equally plausible across likely learner backgrounds.
- [ ] Correct answers do not depend on regional conventions unless relevant.

## Stakes and labeling

- [ ] The instrument is labeled by purpose and stakes.
- [ ] Low-stakes checks are not called tests, exams, or certifications.
- [ ] High-stakes instruments include a human-review gate and appeal path.

## Identity data

- [ ] Demographic questions are optional unless required.
- [ ] Gender fields are not binary unless legally required.
- [ ] Pronoun collection is optional and consent-based.
- [ ] Data retention and use are disclosed.
